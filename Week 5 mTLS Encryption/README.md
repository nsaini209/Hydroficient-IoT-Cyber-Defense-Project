# Week 5 - mTLS Encryption

### This week, I migrated our communication infrastructure from standard TLS to Mutual TLS (mTLS) by enforcing require_certificate: true. While our previous TLS setup provided one-way verification (server-side only), mTLS establishes a cryptographically secure two-way identity challenge. By requiring both the broker and the client to present certificates signed by our private Root CA, we’ve effectively eliminated the risk of unauthorized or 'rogue' devices connecting to the broker. This transition not only hardens our transport layer against MITM attacks but also provides a foundation for Identity-Based Access Control, as we can now verify the specific 'Common Name' of every connecting client before any data exchange occurs.
